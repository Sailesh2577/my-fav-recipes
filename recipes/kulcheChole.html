KADHAI PANEER

INGREDIENTS

For pressure cooking chana

  • 1 cup dried white chickpeas kabuli chana
  • 2 tsp tea leaves
  • 1 black cardamom
  • 2 green cardamoms
  • 1 inch cinnamon
  • 3 cloves
  • 1/4 tsp black salt
  • 3 cup water

For masala mix

  • 1 tbsp red chilli powder
  • 1/4 tsp turmeric powder
  • 1 tsp coriander powder
  • 1 tsp cumin powder
  • 1 tsp amchur powder
  • 1 tsp chole masalas
  • 1/2 tsp garam masala

For chole curry

  • 3 tbsp oil
  • 1 tsp cumin seeds
  • 1 bay leaf
  • 1/2 tsp kasuri methi
  • 1/2 cup chopped onion
  • 1 tbsp ginger-garlic paste
  • 2 green chilli slits
  • Masala mix
  • 3/4 cup or 2 medium size tomato puree
  • Salt to taste
  • Boiled chana
  • 1 cup chana water
  • some coriander leaves
  • 1for chana curry tadka
  • 2 tbsp ghee
  • 1 inch ginger slits
  • 2 green chilli slits
  • 1/4 tsp red chilli powder

For instant plain kulcha

  • 1.5 cup maida
  • 1 tbsp sugar
  • Salt to taste
  • 2 tbsp milk powder
  • 2 tbsp oil
  • 1/2 cup curd/yoghurt
  • 11 packet eno
  • Water as required

Instructions

Soaking and pressure cook chana

  1. Wash the dried chana and Soak them in enough water for at least 8 hours or overnight.
  2. After soaking time, they will get doubled in size. Discard the soaking water.
  3. Now in a cotton cloth, add tea leaves, cinnamon, black cardamom, green cardamom, and cloves. Combine the edges of the cloth and make a potli of it.
  4. Then in a pressure cooker, add soaked chana, black salt, dry spices potli, and water.
  5. Cover the cooker with a lid and pressure cook it for 6-7 whistle on medium flame.
  6. Let the pressure go down by itself then open the lid.
  7. Discard the tea bags and whole spices. now separate boiled chana and water. Keep it aside.

Making chole curry recipe

  1. Firstly, in a mixing bowl, add red chilli powder, turmeric powder, coriander powder, cumin powder, amchur powder, chole masala, and garama masala. mix well. Masala mix is ready.
  2. Now heat the oil in a pan, add cumin seeds, bay leaf, and kasuri methi. Sauté for a minute..
  3. Now add chopped onions and sauté till it gets light brown in color. Also, add ginger-garlic paste and green chilli slits. Sauté for a minute.
  4. Then add masala mix and sauté till oil separate from its sides.
  5. Add tomato puree and salt. Cover and cook gravy till oil release from its sides
  6. Then add boiled chana and mix well. Mash some chana with a bowl and mix well.
  7. Now add boiled chana water and mix well.
  8. Bring it to a simmer and let simmer on low-medium heat for 10-15 minutes.
  9. After simmering, the water gravy will get thicker, and also chana has absorbed lots of flavors.
  10. Heat ghee in a pan, add ginger slits, green chilli slits, and sauté. Switch off the gas, add red chilli powder and add tadka on chole curry. Mix well.
  11. Lastly, sprinkle chopped coriander leaves. chole curry is ready.

Making instant plain kulcha

  1. In a mixing bowl, add maida, sugar, salt, milk powder, oil, and curd. Mix well.
  2. Add eno packet and some water. mix well and knead soft dough.
  3. Cover and rest for 30 minutes or more.
  4. Pinch a ball-sized dough and flatten it.
  5. sprinkle some black sesame seeds and coriander leaves.
  6. gently roll to a round or oval shape using a rolling pin.
  7. Heat tawa, add kulcha on it, sprinkle some water on its sides. Cover and cook kulcha for 5 minutes on medium flame.
  8. Flip the side, apply some ghee and cook kulcha from both sides.
  9. finally, serve plain kulcha hot with chole curry.
